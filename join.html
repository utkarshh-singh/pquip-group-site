Join the PQUIP Group

We welcome motivated students and postdocs with backgrounds in physics, photonics, EE, CS/ML, or related fields. We combine theory, simulation, and experiment across quantum communications, light–matter interfaces, quantum simulation, and photonic quantum information processing.

Open Tracks

Postdoctoral Researchers

  • Background in quantum optics, photonics, quantum information, or ML for quantum.
  • Lead subprojects; mentor students; publish and present.
  • Start: Rolling (typically 3–12 months lead time).

Please mention your earliest start date and two recent representative papers.

PhD Students

  • Strong coursework/research in physics, photonics, EE, or CS/ML.
  • Experience with one of: quantum optics labs, simulations, or ML.
  • Start: Fall/Winter; applications reviewed year-round.

Include unofficial transcripts; highlight any publications or preprints.

MSc / MEng Students

  • Excellent undergraduate preparation in a relevant field.
  • Thesis or project options available depending on fit.
  • Start: Fall/Winter; reviewed on a rolling basis.

Tell us which research area(s) excite you and any prior lab/software experience.

Undergrad / Visiting Interns

  • Research terms (NSERC/USRA, co-op, summer), 8–16+ weeks.
  • Good programming or lab skills are a plus.
  • Start: Summer/Fall; apply 3–5 months ahead.

Attach an unofficial transcript and a short paragraph about your interests.

How to Apply

  1. Prepare a single PDF with:
    • CV (with publications and links, if any)
    • Statement of interest (1–2 pages): background, interests, and how you see your fit
    • Unofficial transcripts (for student roles)
  2. Email your application to placeholder@example.com with the subject:
    Application – [Postdoc | PhD | MSc | Intern] – Your Name
  3. Optional: include links to code (GitHub), preprints, or posters.

Selection & Timing

We review applications continuously and invite shortlisted candidates for a brief call followed by an interview. Typical timelines are 2–6 weeks depending on the role and season.

Funding & Support

Positions are fully funded. Competitive scholarships and fellowships are encouraged; we will support strong candidates for relevant programs.

Environment

We are co-located across the National Research Council of Canada and the University of Ottawa with access to excellent photonics and computation facilities.

FAQ

Do I need prior quantum experience?

No. We value core strengths (e.g., optics, control, computing/ML, algorithms) and a willingness to learn. Tell us how your skills transfer.

Can I apply without publications?

Yes. For student roles, coursework, projects, or open-source work are great signals. Share links if applicable.

Where are you located?

Ottawa, Canada — primarily at the National Research Council of Canada and the University of Ottawa.

Equity, Diversity & Inclusion

We are committed to a respectful, inclusive environment. We encourage applications from all qualified candidates, including individuals from under-represented groups.
