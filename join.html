Open Tracks

Postdoctoral Researchers
  • PhD (completed/near completion) in physics, maths or related fields.
  • Lead subprojects, publish, present, and mentor junior members.
  • Funding: NRC/NSERC grants, Banting, Mitacs Elevate, etc.
  • Start: Rolling, typically 3–12 months lead time.
PhD Students
  • BSc + MSc, or direct-entry after BSc with strong GPA/research.
  • Apply through the uOttawa Physics graduate program.
  • Funding: guaranteed stipend, RA/TA support, NSERC/OGS/Vanier awards encouraged.
  • Start: Fall/Winter, reviewed year-round.
MSc / MEng Students
  • Bachelor’s in physics, maths or related fields.
  • MSc: thesis-based (PhD track)
  • Funding: stipend for MSc; MEng usually self-funded.
  • Start: Fall/Winter intake; rolling consideration.
Undergrad / Visiting Interns
  • BSc students via NSERC USRA, Mitacs Globalink, co-op, or summer internships.
  • Duration: 4–16 weeks.
  • Good programming or lab skills are a plus, but not required.
  • Start: Summer/Fall, apply 3–5 months ahead.

How to Apply

  1. Prepare PDF files for:
    • CV (with publications and links, if any)
    • Statement of interest (1–2 pages): background, interests, and how you see your fit
    • Unofficial transcripts (for student roles)
  2. Email your application to placeholder@example.com with the subject:
    Application – [Postdoc | PhD | MSc | Intern] – Your Name
  3. Optional: include links to code (GitHub), preprints, or posters.

Selection & Timing

We review applications continuously and invite shortlisted candidates for a brief talk to explain their work. Typical timelines are 2–6 weeks, depending on the role and season.

Funding & Support

Positions are fully funded. Competitive scholarships and fellowships are encouraged; we will support strong candidates for relevant programs.

Environment

We are co-located across the National Research Council of Canada and the University of Ottawa, with access to excellent computation facilities.

FAQ

Do I need prior quantum experience?

No. We value core strengths (e.g., optics, control, computing/ML, algorithms) and a willingness to learn. Tell us how your skills transfer.

Can I apply without publications?

Yes. For student roles, coursework, projects, or open-source work are great signals. Share links if applicable.

Where are you located?

Ottawa, Canada — primarily at the National Research Council of Canada and the University of Ottawa.

Equity, Diversity & Inclusion

We are committed to a respectful, inclusive environment. We encourage applications from all qualified candidates, including individuals from under-represented groups.
